Pizza Fresca
This logo was made for a restaurant located on UCLA's campus. The concept of the restaurant was to provide students with healthy, organic, personal sized pizzas. Its menu includes standard pizzas as well as gluten free and whole-wheat options. While working on this project
I wanted to make sure that the logo reflected the organic and healthy attributes that the pizza has. I did this through the use of typography, color, and texture.
UCLA Homecoming 2011
The UCLA homecoming logo was used to visually represent the celebrations of the 2011 homecoming weekend. The logo was put on flyers that advertised a carnival and football game as well as t-shirts, and other marketing opportunities. The outline that appears on the logo is of the Royce Hall building, which is an iconic image of UCLA.
Ackerman's 50th Anniversary
In the spring of 2011, UCLA celebrated the 50th anniversary of the William C. Ackerman Student Union. The logo was used on the timeline displays, light pole banners, and floor decals, in order to commemorate Ackerman Union's 50 years on the campus. The shape of the logo incorporated the arches that are found in the architecture of the building as well as uses UCLA's colors, blue and yellow.
USAC President's Office Logo
As the Director of Design for the Undergraduate Student Association Council (USAC) President's Office, I designed the logo for the office. The logo is used on flyers and other promotional items that advertise the office's involvement in different campus wide events.

Fusion
Fusion is a restaurant on UCLA's campus in the Court of Sciences Student Center. This restaurant has a rotating menu that changes between Korean BBQ, Mediterranean, Indian, Argentinean, and Vietnamese food. I designed the main logo as well as three of the rotating menu logos. The restaurant is located next to the science and laboratory buildings are on campus so the design of the Fusion logo plays off the idea of fusing atoms as well as the idea of the movement that mirrors the changing menus.
Bombshelter Bistro
Bombshelter Bistro is a restaurant on UCLA's campus in the Court of Sciences Student Center. Its menu will cater to the customers that come from the on campus hospital and provide them with a higher end dining option that resembles a restaurant more than a food court. When working on this logo I wanted to embody the freshness of the food they would be serving, as well as the more modern, high-end price of their menu.

How to Cry Underwater
How to Cry Underwater is a book that I wrote, illustrated, laid out, designed, and bound in the spring of 2011. The book is a satirical and humorous instructional guide on how avoid crying by simply doing the deed while under some water. While creating the graphics for the book I explored how to reduce images to the simplest details while still communicating what is happening. In the process of creating the physical book, I also played around with textures by printing on transparent paper and making the warning signs into stickers.

Under the Shell
Perfection is a concept that I am consistently trying to define. However, one attribute that I find is always constant is that it is used a shield. I have found that is so much easier to hide behind the idea of perfection than it is to confront and uncover the truth. Through the image of the dead chick I am uncovering the insecurities that have been hidden away.

A Self Act of Isolation
This series of images represents my inability to communicate due to a fear of confrontation. I fear confrontation because of how I observed its affect on the people around me while I was growing up and I never wanted to put that same negative toll on my own relationships. As I grew up I began to notice how my quiet, non-confrontational personality was isolating me from society because I found it was easier to keep my feelings to myself than to talk to them about them. These images all contain an object that is coated with a sticky, gross substance. This coating represents its separation from society as well as a defect in its existence.

ABOUT

Mary is currently a student studying Design Media Arts at UCLA. She started out with a love for photography, but since coming to UCLA has found a passion for graphic design, and more specifically logo design. Mary is a detail-oriented person and sees logo design as a way of combining the right details to make a perfect visual identity.
Some of Mary's accomplishments include receiving recognition from UCLA's Design Media Arts program as being a top student her Freshman year, designing a shirt that was sold on Bloomingdales.com, and designing a number of logos that that are used on UCLA's campus.
